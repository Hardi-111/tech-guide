7 Simple Things to Check Before Buying A Pen Drive


Who would have believed this if we had told them about the functions of a pen drive let’s just say a few decades back! Isn’t it amazing how much data you can load onto such a tiny device? Pen drive was invented in 1998 by IBM. Thanks to them, we no longer have to go through the painstaking process of burning CDs or reading through the labels of all the floppy disks in the drawer to copy a movie or store our photos. Pen drives or USB flash drives are basically data storage devices that are typically removable and rewritable. If you want one for your office or for yourself, we can help you get the best.
Below is the checklist you need to follow before buying a pen drive.
1. The reason why you need them
Before you make a purchase, it is always worthwhile to assess your needs. Like the kind of data that you intend to store in the device, your budget, whether you will need to transfer files often, how will you use them, and more. Or do you want to purchase one so that you can gift it to someone in the office? Requirements of each person vary. Make sure to list them out and take your time to analyze each. 2. Storage capacity — the more, the merrier.
Your storage needs totally depend on the kind of files you want to save or transfer. Keep in mind that video and image files need more space than text files. A 1GB flash drive can save up to 20,000 pages long text files, 1000 images, 500 minutes of music or 45 minutes of a high-resolution movie. Just take a look at the file details on your computer to get an estimate about how much space you will need.

3. Transfer speed
Before we venture into which transfer speed you should go for, you should learn about the two types of USB flash drives available in the market. They are the 2.0 pen drive and 3.0 pen drive. 2.0 pen drive transfers 60 MB data per second whereas 3.0 pen drive transfers 640 MB data per second. That is more than 10 times! But it comes with a price, of course. 3.0 pen drives are costlier than the former. They are also not compatible with all the devices out there that you may end up underutilizing its capabilities. Even if you plug-in a 3.0 pen drive on a computer that is incompatible, it functions like a 2.0 pen drive. 5. Size
In the case of pen drives, the rule always is — the small, the better. They are usually around 2 inches long. You can also find them in different styles and shapes that match your personality. But always remember that they should be small enough to be easily carried around in the pocket or tied to a key chain. But they should not be too small that you lose them out of sight. If they come with a cap to protect the metal end from damage or with a foldable design, that would be awesome.
